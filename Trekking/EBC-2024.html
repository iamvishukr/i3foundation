About the Trek

The Annapurna Base Camp trek is one of the most popular treks in Nepal. It offers stunning views of the Himalayas, diverse landscapes, and a chance to experience the local culture.

7 Days Annapurna Base Camp Trekking Day-to-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Kathmandu

Arrive in Kathmandu, transfer to the hotel, and prepare for the trek. Explore the vibrant streets of Thamel and enjoy a welcome dinner.

Day 2: Fly to Pokhara and Trek to Tikhedhunga

Take a scenic flight to Pokhara, drive to Nayapul, and start the trek to Tikhedhunga. Enjoy the lush green landscapes.

Day 3: Trek to Ghorepani

Trek through rhododendron forests and reach Ghorepani. Enjoy stunning views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to trek to Annapurna Base Camp?

The best time to trek to Annapurna Base Camp is during the pre-monsoon (March to May) and post-monsoon (September to November) seasons. These months offer clear skies and stable weather.

What is the difficulty level of the trek?

The Annapurna Base Camp trek is considered moderate in difficulty. It involves walking for 5-6 hours daily on varied terrain, including steep ascents and descents.

What kind of accommodation is available during the trek?

Accommodation during the trek is primarily in teahouses or lodges. These provide basic facilities such as a bed, blanket, and shared bathrooms.

Are meals included in the trek?

Yes, meals are included. You will be provided with breakfast, lunch, and dinner during the trek. The meals are typically Nepali or continental.

What should I pack for the trek?

You should pack warm clothing, sturdy trekking boots, a sleeping bag, a first aid kit, sunscreen, and other personal essentials. A detailed packing list will be provided upon booking.
